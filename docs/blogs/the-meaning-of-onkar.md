# The Meaning of Onkar

## What It Means

What Does "Onkar" Mean?
The name Onkar is derived from the Sanskrit word: ॐकार (Onkar / Omkar)
It means: "The embodiment of the sacred sound Om (ॐ)"
At its core, Onkar represents the ultimate reality, universal consciousness, and the origin of existence.

So when someone is named Onkar, it signifies: A person connected to the essence of existence itself.

Onkar in Indian Spiritual Traditions:
Hindu Philosophy: In Hinduism, Onkar is closely linked to the idea of Brahman—the ultimate, formless reality. Ancient texts describe "Om" as the sound that encompasses everything in the universe.
Sikhism: In Sikh philosophy, the phrase "Ik Onkar" means "There is only one God". Here, Onkar represents unity, oneness, and the universal creator.
