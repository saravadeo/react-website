# AI News Roundup — May 15, 2026

The AI industry's center of gravity shifted this week. Anthropic — long seen as OpenAI's scrappy younger sibling — is now in talks for a valuation that would make it the most valuable AI company on Earth. Google killed the Chromebook and replaced it with something AI-native. SAP declared the end of traditional ERP and replaced it with autonomous agents. And the cybersecurity fallout from Anthropic's Mythos model has US banks in emergency patching mode. Meanwhile, the AI restructuring wave is turning into a tsunami of layoffs. Here's your daily briefing.

## 💰 Anthropic in Talks for $950B Valuation — Would Eclipse OpenAI

According to a bombshell **New York Times** report on May 12, Anthropic is in early talks with investors to raise **$30–50 billion** at a valuation as high as **$950 billion**. Bloomberg separately reported the figure as more than **$900 billion**, with the round expected to close as soon as the end of May.

### The Numbers Are Staggering

If finalized, Anthropic's valuation would jump **2.5× from the $380 billion** it was valued at just three months ago in February. That's a **1,445% increase in one year**. The round would also make Anthropic larger than OpenAI's latest reported valuation of **$825 billion** — a stunning reversal for a company that was widely considered the runner-up.

### What's Driving It

CEO **Dario Amodei** recently disclosed that Anthropic has surged to a **$30 billion revenue run rate**, and claimed the company could grow **80× as big** this year. The momentum comes from multiple fronts:

- **Mythos**, the cybersecurity-focused model released last month, demonstrated capabilities so powerful that the Fed chair and Treasury secretary convened emergency meetings with bank CEOs
- **Enterprise adoption**: For the first time, more business users are using Anthropic's AI than OpenAI's, according to spend management platform Ramp
- **Strategic deals**: Google committed up to **$40 billion** and Amazon up to **$25 billion** in separate investment agreements last month
- **SpaceX computing deal**: Anthropic signed an agreement with SpaceX to use the Colossus GPU cluster to boost Claude's computing power

### Why It Matters

The valuation talks signal that investors believe the AI market isn't a winner-take-all game — or at least that the "winner" may not be who everyone assumed. Anthropic's focus on safety, its enterprise partnerships with Blackstone and Goldman Sachs, and its cybersecurity capabilities with Mythos are creating a fundamentally different value proposition from OpenAI's consumer-first approach.

## 💻 Google Kills the Chromebook, Introduces AI-Native Googlebook

At its **Android Show: I/O Edition** event on May 12, Google didn't just update Android — it redefined its hardware strategy. The biggest announcement: **Googlebook**, a new category of laptops designed from the ground up for **Gemini Intelligence**, replacing the Chromebook brand that defined Google's laptop strategy for over a decade.

### What Is Googlebook?

Googlebook is built around **Gemini Intelligence** — Google's new umbrella brand for its best AI features across phones, watches, cars, glasses, and now laptops. The first Googlebooks are expected this fall, and they feature a radical new interaction model called **Magic Pointer**, built with Google DeepMind, that brings Gemini's assistance directly to the cursor.

### Gemini Intelligence Across Android

The event showcased how deeply Google is embedding AI into the Android ecosystem:

- **Cross-app agentic actions**: Gemini can take data from one app and perform multistep functions across apps
- **Gemini in Chrome for Android**: Summarize web pages, ask questions about content you're viewing
- **Vibe-coded widgets**: A "Create My Widget" feature lets users generate custom homescreen widgets using natural language
- **Gboard Rambler**: Gemini-powered dictation that turns speech into cleaned-up, formatted text
- **Personal Intelligence autofill**: Opt-in feature to help users fill out complex forms using personal context

### Why It Matters

Google is making its biggest bet yet that AI isn't a feature — it's the operating system. By replacing the Chromebook with Googlebook, Google is signaling that the "browser-first" computing era is over, and the "AI-first" era has arrived. The tight integration between Gemini Intelligence and Android hardware could give Google a vertical stack advantage that neither Apple nor Microsoft can easily match.

## 🏢 SAP Declares the Autonomous Enterprise at Sapphire 2026

At **SAP Sapphire Orlando 2026** (May 12–13), CEO **Christian Klein** unveiled what SAP calls its most aggressive repositioning in a generation: **the Autonomous Enterprise**. The message was clear — the era of traditional ERP, where humans interact with software through interfaces, is ending. The future is AI agents that execute business processes end-to-end.

### Key Announcements

1. **SAP Business AI Platform**: Unifies SAP Business Technology Platform, SAP Business Data Cloud, and SAP Business AI into a single governed environment, anchored by a new **SAP Knowledge Graph** that contains information about all business entities, processes, and relationships
2. **SAP Autonomous Suite**: Enables SAP applications to execute processes themselves, with governance as the critical trust layer
3. **50+ domain-specific Joule agents**: Covering finance, HR, procurement, and supply chain
4. **Joule Studio 2.0**: A new development environment for building custom AI agents
5. **AI Agent Hub**: A marketplace for third-party agents, with governance controls
6. **Joule Work**: The next evolution of user experience — people set direction, AI executes
7. **Predictive AI with RPT-1.5**: SAP's new predictive model for business forecasting

### The Enterprise Stack Play

SAP is betting that the winning layer in enterprise AI isn't the model — it's **governance**. As Klein stated: "We will infuse this deep domain know-how into the AI agents for accurate, compliant and secure outcomes." The Knowledge Graph provides the operational context, process logic, and compliance infrastructure that determines whether an autonomous agent can be trusted with a financial close, a procurement approval, or a supply chain decision worth millions.

Microsoft also announced deeper SAP-on-Azure integrations, connecting SAP's Business AI Platform and Joule with Microsoft Teams, Fabric, and Copilot.

### Why It Matters

If SAP executes on this vision, it fundamentally changes how enterprises operate. Instead of humans running reports, approving workflows, and managing exceptions, AI agents handle operational work while people set strategy and direction. Forbes called it "the end of the ERP era" — and SAP seems comfortable with that framing. The question is whether 50 years of enterprise muscle memory can be rewritten by AI agents in a few years.

## 🔒 Mythos Fallout: US Banks in Emergency Patching Mode

The cybersecurity crisis triggered by Anthropic's **Mythos** model continues to escalate. A **Reuters** report on May 12 revealed that US banks are rushing to fix scores of IT system weaknesses flagged by the powerful AI tool, prompting urgent repairs, software upgrades, and raising the possibility of disruption for customers.

### The Scale of the Problem

- A handful of the country's largest lenders currently have access to Mythos through Anthropic's **Project Glasswing** initiative and about 40 additional organizations
- Mythos found **thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities** across every major operating system and web browser
- Anthropic CEO **Dario Amodei** described the current period as a "**moment of danger**" and warned of "some enormous increase in the amount of vulnerabilities, in the amount of breaches, in the financial damage that's done from ransomware on schools, hospitals, not to mention banks"
- The company estimates a **six-to-twelve month window** to patch the flaws before adversaries build models that can do the same thing

### Banking Industry Response

JPMorgan CEO **Jamie Dimon** previously acknowledged that while AI tools could eventually help companies defend themselves, they are "first making them more vulnerable." Banks are now conducting emergency security assessments, accelerating patch cycles, and in some cases upgrading entire software stacks. The disruption could affect customer-facing systems.

### CNBC Analysis: The Threat Was Already Here

A May 8 CNBC analysis made a critical point: the vulnerabilities Mythos found **already existed**. Cybersecurity experts confirmed that the same flaws can be found using existing models from both Anthropic and OpenAI. Mythos didn't create the danger — it simply revealed the scale of it. The implication is sobering: if friendly AI can find these bugs, hostile AI isn't far behind.

### Why It Matters

This is no longer a hypothetical risk. Anthropic has effectively started a countdown: the world has 6–12 months to fix thousands of critical vulnerabilities before AI-powered adversaries can exploit them at scale. Banks are just the first domino — critical infrastructure including power grids, water systems, and healthcare networks face the same exposure.

## 📉 AI Layoff Wave Accelerates: 25,000 Cut in Two Weeks

The AI restructuring wave that began as a trickle is now a flood. In just the first two weeks of May 2026, approximately **25,000 tech employees** were laid off as companies race to rebuild around AI.

### The Major Cuts

- **Cloudflare**: 1,100 employees (20% of workforce) — the first mass layoff in the company's 16-year history, despite posting record Q1 revenue. CEO Matthew Prince called it a restructuring around an "agentic AI-first operating model." Internal AI usage surged 600% in three months
- **Upwork**: 25% of workforce cut as the freelance platform restructures around AI
- **Coinbase**: Significant cuts as part of AI-driven operational overhaul
- **LinkedIn** (Microsoft subsidiary): 5% of staff (approximately 875 employees) laid off
- **BILL Holdings**: Layoffs as part of AI restructuring

### The Irony

Cloudflare's situation encapsulates the paradox of 2026: the company had **record revenue** and **beat analyst estimates**, yet still cut 20% of its workforce. As Prince wrote: "Today's actions are not a cost-cutting exercise or an assessment of individuals' performance; they are about Cloudflare defining how a world-class, high-growth company operates and creates value in the agentic AI era."

Layoffs are reportedly **up 43% from last year**, and industry watchers expect the trend to continue as more companies attempt to become "leaner" through AI adoption.

### Why It Matters

We're past the point where AI layoffs are about cutting costs during downturns. These are structural reductions — companies genuinely believe they can operate with fewer humans because AI can handle the work. Whether that belief is justified remains to be seen, but the human cost is real and immediate.

## 🔧 OpenAI-Broadcom $18B Chip Deal Hits Financing Snag

The **$18 billion custom chip deal** between OpenAI and Broadcom — code-named **"Project Nexus"** — has stalled over a financing dispute, causing Broadcom shares to drop roughly 4%.

### What Happened

Broadcom will only finance the first phase of chip production — requiring **1.3 gigawatts of data center capacity** — if **Microsoft agrees to buy roughly 40% of the chips**. Microsoft has not committed, citing infrastructure disagreements. The deal was supposed to help OpenAI deploy up to **10 gigawatts of custom AI accelerators**, with Broadcom starting rack deployments in the second half of 2026.

### Why It Matters

The snag highlights a fundamental tension in the AI infrastructure buildout: chipmakers want guaranteed demand before committing to massive capital expenditures, while cloud providers and AI companies want flexibility. If this deal collapses, it could slow OpenAI's push toward AI hardware independence and keep the company dependent on Nvidia for longer than planned.
