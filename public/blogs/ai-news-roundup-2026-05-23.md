## 🔒 Project Glasswing: Anthropic's Mythos Finds 10,000+ Critical Vulnerabilities

Anthropic released the first major update to Project Glasswing, its initiative to use frontier AI to secure critical open-source software — and the numbers are staggering.

Launched on April 7, 2026, Project Glasswing gives key technology providers early access to **Claude Mythos Preview**, Anthropic's most capable model yet for security research. In just one month, Mythos has identified **over 10,000 critical vulnerabilities** across major open-source platforms, including high-severity flaws in Firefox, Linux kernel components, and widely-used encryption libraries.

Key findings from the Glasswing update:

- **Firefox alone** had 10x more vulnerabilities discovered than previously known, including previously unknown exploit chains
- Mythos can **autonomously discover and exploit** software vulnerabilities at a scale and speed that surpasses all but the most skilled human security researchers
- Partners including **Microsoft, CrowdStrike, and Google** have joined as founding members of the Glasswing security consortium
- The model **collapses the gap** between vulnerability discovery and exploitation — meaning attackers with similar capabilities could weaponize flaws in near-real-time

The UK's NCSC has already warned defenders to prepare for a world where frontier AI amplifies attacker capabilities at pace. IANS Faculty researchers note that Glasswing represents a **temporary advantage for defenders**, and organizations must compress patch timelines and prepare for near-immediate weaponization as similar AI capabilities spread to adversaries.

Anthropic is tightly restricting access to Mythos specifically because of its dual-use potential — the same capability that finds bugs can also exploit them.

## 🚀 SpaceX S-1 Filing Reveals $45 Billion Anthropic Compute Deal

SpaceX officially filed its S-1 with the SEC on May 20, 2026, kicking off what could become the largest IPO in history at a **$2 trillion valuation**. Buried in the filing was a revelation that sent shockwaves through the AI industry: **Anthropic is paying SpaceX $1.25 billion per month** for compute capacity through May 2029 — totaling over **$45 billion** over the life of the deal.

The deal covers:

- **Full capacity** of SpaceX's Colossus 1 data center in Memphis, Tennessee
- Additional compute from the newer **Colossus 2** data center
- Capacity scaling up in May and June 2026

The irony is thick: Elon Musk, who merged SpaceX with his AI startup xAI earlier this year, is now renting his rival's compute infrastructure to Anthropic — the company he publicly criticized during the Musk v. Altman trial. And xAI's own Grok model was reportedly using only about **11% of Colossus 1's capacity**, making Anthropic the tenant that actually fills the building.

The deal also raises questions about compute concentration. Anthropic now has massive compute agreements with **Amazon** (up to $25 billion), **Google Cloud** ($200 billion commitment), and now SpaceX — an unprecedented infrastructure moat for AI training and inference.

SpaceX's IPO filing also revealed a Mars-focused incentive structure: Musk's performance shares include a milestone requiring a **permanent human colony on Mars with at least one million inhabitants**.

## 💼 LinkedIn Cuts 900 Jobs (5% of Workforce) Despite Record Revenue

Microsoft-owned LinkedIn announced it is laying off approximately **900 employees** — roughly 5% of its global workforce — even as the platform posted **12% year-on-year revenue growth** and crossed **$5 billion in quarterly revenue** for the first time.

LinkedIn CEO Tom Shapero cited a need to restructure teams around AI and reduce "underutilized office space," including shutting down the company's office in Graz, Austria. The layoffs primarily affect engineering, product, and talent acquisition teams.

The move highlights a growing paradox in Big Tech: **companies are generating record revenue while simultaneously cutting headcount**. LinkedIn's parent Microsoft laid off 6,000 employees earlier this year, including its AI Director — underscoring that even AI-focused roles aren't immune.

This isn't just a LinkedIn story. Meta kicked off 2026 by cutting 1,500 Reality Labs employees in January, then another **8,000 jobs** in May as it pivots aggressively toward AI. The broader 2026 tech layoff wave has seen 55% of U.S. hiring managers expect further cuts, with 44% pointing to AI as a top driver.

## 🗣️ Anthropic Widens the Conversation on Frontier AI

On May 19, Anthropic published a significant essay titled **"Widening the Conversation on Frontier AI,"** outlining a new initiative to engage scholars, philosophers, clergy, and ethicists in shaping the values and behaviors embedded in Claude.

The key points:

- Anthropic has been holding **dialogues with diverse voices** — not just technologists — about what a "flourishing future" looks like in a world of powerful AI
- These conversations will inform practical decisions about **Claude's constitution**, the values it's trained to embody, and the range of behaviors Anthropic evaluates
- The initiative acknowledges that AI systems interacting with millions of people carry enormous responsibility, and that **technical AI safety alone is insufficient** — the industry needs broader societal input
- Anthropic is also sharing more about the development process for frontier AI systems and their societal impacts

This represents a meaningful shift for a company that has historically been perceived as insular. It also comes at a time when Anthropic is rapidly scaling — between Project Glasswing, the SpaceX compute deal, and its widening enterprise footprint, the company is making the case that responsible scaling requires not just better models, but better conversations about what those models should value.

## 📊 The Big Picture

This week crystallized three trends that will define the rest of 2026:

1. **Compute is the new oil.** Anthropic's $45B SpaceX deal, $200B Google commitment, and Amazon partnership together represent the largest infrastructure buildout any single AI company has ever assembled. The question isn't whether Anthropic can afford it — it's whether the returns will justify the unprecedented capital expenditure.

2. **AI security is a two-sided coin.** Project Glasswing proves that frontier AI can be humanity's best defense against software vulnerabilities — and also its most dangerous potential weapon. The window where defenders have the advantage is closing.

3. **The AI transition has a human cost.** LinkedIn cutting 900 jobs while posting record revenue, Meta shedding 8,000+ workers for an AI pivot, and Microsoft letting go of its own AI Director — these aren't isolated incidents. They're the leading edge of a structural shift that policymakers are only beginning to grapple with.

California's SB 53, signed last September, remains the only enforceable frontier AI regulation in the U.S. Federal action remains stalled after Trump's last-minute reversal of his own executive order. The industry is building faster than the guardrails.
