## Pope Leo XIV Releases *Magnifica Humanitas* — First Papal Encyclical on AI

On May 25, Pope Leo XIV released his first encyclical, *Magnifica Humanitas* ("Magnificent Humanity"), focused on preserving the human person in the age of artificial intelligence. It is the first papal encyclical ever to address AI directly — and the choice of topic itself is a statement.

The encyclical was presented at the Vatican's Synod Hall with a notable group of speakers: Cardinal Víctor Manuel Fernández, Cardinal Michael Czerny, theologian Professor Anna Rowlands, **Christopher Olah** (co-founder of Anthropic and head of interpretability research), and Professor Leocadie Lushombo of Santa Clara University. The inclusion of Olah — an AI researcher at a Vatican encyclical presentation — signals how seriously the Church is taking engagement with the actual builders of this technology.

Based on the Pope's earlier comments, the encyclical is expected to address:
- The **sacred character of the human face and voice** — and what it means when machines can replicate them
- The **dignity of labor** — work as human development, not reducible to economic output
- **Protecting children** from AI products that "pretend to be their friend" while isolating or manipulating them
- A **politics of the common good** that regulates markets and technology to serve everyone, not just the powerful

The Future of Life Institute called it "a hopeful sign and may even prove to be a turning-point" in efforts to steer AI toward benefiting all life. The encyclical also comes on the anniversary of *Rerum Novarum*, Leo XIII's 1891 encyclical on workers' rights — a deliberate echo that frames AI's disruption of labor in a century-old tradition of Catholic social teaching.

Vice President JD Vance reflected that a pope speaking into the age of technology could be as pivotal as Pope Leo XIII's guidance during the industrial revolution. Whether the tech industry listens is another question entirely.

## OpenAI Prepares to File for IPO — Targeting Up to $1 Trillion Valuation

On May 20, CNBC and the New York Times reported that OpenAI is preparing to **file a confidential S-1** with the SEC, laying the groundwork for what could become the largest IPO in history — potentially targeting a valuation of **up to $1 trillion**.

The timing was no accident. Just two days earlier, on May 18, a federal jury in Oakland **unanimously rejected Elon Musk's lawsuit** against OpenAI, ruling that Musk waited too long to file his claims that the company betrayed its nonprofit mission. The verdict cleared a massive legal obstacle from OpenAI's path to public markets.

Key details:
- OpenAI has disclosed a **$122 billion funding round** at an **$852 billion post-money valuation**
- The company says it is generating **$2 billion in revenue per month**
- The revised Microsoft partnership — previously one of the biggest structural questions around a listing — has been updated, removing another overhang
- A public S-1 is expected later this year, with a potential **September 2026 IPO date**

But questions remain. As TECHi noted, the real question isn't "when can investors buy OpenAI stock?" — it's whether the business can grow into a valuation that private investors have already marked like a mega-cap platform. With increasing competition from Anthropic and Google, the public market will be the ultimate judge.

## Anthropic Projects First Operating Profit in Q2 2026 — $10.9B Revenue

In a milestone that dropped the same day as OpenAI's IPO news, Anthropic told investors it is on track for its **first operating profit ever** in Q2 2026, with revenue projected at **$10.9 billion** — more than doubling Q1's $4.8 billion in a single quarter.

The numbers are staggering:
- **$10.9B Q2 revenue** — up from $4.8B in Q1
- **~$559M operating profit** — the first quarter Anthropic covers its own costs without relying on outside capital
- **Claude Code surpassed $1B in annualized revenue** within six months of launch, driven by enterprise developers
- Dario Amodei recently said Anthropic had planned to grow 10x this year, but reached a growth rate that could make it **80x as big**

Anthropic raised a **$30 billion Series G** in February 2026 at a **$380 billion post-money valuation**. The path to profitability is critical for an IPO path — and the SpaceX S-1 revealed Anthropic is spending **$1.25 billion/month** on compute from SpaceX's Colossus supercomputer. The question now is whether the profitability is sustainable or whether it's a single quarter driven by extraordinary growth that may normalize.

## Google I/O 2026: Gemini 3.5 Flash, Omni, Spark, and the Agent Takeover

Google I/O 2026 was arguably the most consequential developer conference Google has held in years. Instead of incremental updates, Google unveiled a highly specialized trio of tools and bet the entire company on AI agents.

**Gemini 3.5 Flash** becomes the new default model for the Gemini app and AI Mode in Search globally. It features a 1M token context window and 4x faster inference, outperforming most frontier models on key benchmarks including Gemini 3.1 Pro.

**Gemini Omni** is Google's new "world model" — not just a text-to-video generator, but a multimodal model that understands and generates content across formats. It combines an intuitive understanding of physics with real-world knowledge, so outputs behave like the real world, not just look photorealistic. Gemini Omni Flash launched this week for AI Plus, Pro, and Ultra subscribers, and is rolling out free on YouTube Shorts and YouTube Create.

**Gemini Spark** is easily the most ambitious product revealed — a proactive AI agent that doesn't just respond to prompts but takes initiative across Google's ecosystem. Spark represents Google's answer to the agent race, competing directly with OpenAI's Codex and Anthropic's Claude Code.

Other announcements:
- **Google Antigravity 2.0** — described as "the Xcode for AI agents," a full development environment for building autonomous agents
- **Universal Cart** — an AI-powered shopping agent using the new AP2 protocol that can browse, compare, and purchase across retailers
- **AI Studio app generation** — generate full Android apps from a single prompt
- **Android XR smart glasses** launching Fall 2026
- **AI Ultra subscription** price cut to $200/month, with a lower-cost $99/month tier
- **Managed Agents API** on Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform for instantly spinning up custom agents
- **AI Content Detection API** for identifying synthetically generated media

Google's message was clear: the future isn't chatbots — it's agents.

## Meta Lays Off 8,000, Reassigns 7,000 in Sweeping AI Restructuring

On May 20, Meta executed a brutal workforce overhaul: **8,000 employees laid off** (roughly 10% of the company) and **7,000 more reassigned** to AI-focused roles. The restructuring was telegraphed in an April memo from Chief People Officer Janelle Gale, but the actual impact was still jarring.

The cuts are part of Zuckerberg's effort to remake Meta around AI tools and autonomous agents. The company plans to increase AI spending by **at least 60% in 2026**, with capital expenditures between **$115 billion and $135 billion** this year — much of it on AI chips, data centers, and strategic acquisitions including the AI agent platform Moltbook.

The layoffs came alongside organizational flattening — eliminating layers of management — and a controversial new program to track employee data for AI training, which prompted workers to hang petitions on office walls.

Meta's restructuring is part of a broader pattern across tech: Microsoft, Block, and Coinbase have all recently announced AI-driven reorganizations. The message from every corner of the industry is the same: if you're not building AI, you're expendable.

## Musk v OpenAI: Jury Unanimously Rejects Elon Musk's Lawsuit

On May 18, a nine-person federal jury in Oakland delivered a **unanimous verdict** against Elon Musk in his lawsuit against OpenAI, Sam Altman, and Greg Brockman. The jury found that Musk waited too long to sue — missing the statute of limitations — and dismissed all claims.

The three-week trial was one of the most dramatic courtroom spectacles in tech history. Musk accused Altman and Brockman of manipulating him into contributing $38 million, then going behind his back by converting OpenAI from a nonprofit to a for-profit entity and accepting tens of billions from Microsoft. Altman, Brockman, Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella, and Musk himself all testified.

The jury didn't buy it. U.S. District Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers accepted the verdict and dismissed the claims. Musk called it a "technicality" and vowed to appeal — but the path forward is narrow. The verdict effectively removes the biggest legal cloud over OpenAI's IPO plans, which moved forward within 48 hours.
