# AI News Roundup — May 17, 2026

The AI world is holding its breath before Google I/O 2026, Anthropic is closing in on an eye-popping valuation, and Meta's next model is nowhere to be found. Here's everything that matters this Saturday.

---

## 🌐 Google I/O 2026 — 48 Hours and Counting

Google I/O kicks off **Monday, May 19 at 10am PT** from Shoreline Amphitheatre in Mountain View, with simultaneous livestreaming at [io.google](https://io.google). The Android Show on May 12 already front-loaded platform announcements — leaving the main stage for model releases, developer tooling, and hardware reveals.

### What's Confirmed

- **Gemini 4.0 (or named variant)** — Google confirmed "the latest Gemini model updates" and "agentic coding" on the keynote agenda. A flagship release is widely expected, with upgrades to multimodal reasoning, longer context windows for Workspace, and improved agentic reliability for the Cloud agent toolkit.
- **Android XR Glasses Preview** — Google will preview Android XR glasses at I/O. Hardware partnerships with Samsung, Warby Parker, Gentle Monster, and XREAL suggest multiple form factors. A display-free model (hands-free Gemini interaction) is confirmed for 2026; a display-equipped in-lens overlay model has not been confirmed for this year. Samsung's Galaxy Glasses are expected to appear alongside Google's own hardware.
- **Aluminium OS** — Google's Android-based PC operating system for the consumer laptop market is confirmed for a 2026 launch. A leaked 16-minute hands-on video showed an Android-style desktop interface with a bottom dock, virtual desktops, and a "Link to iOS" app for iPhone interoperability. I/O may bring hardware partnership announcements alongside the OS.
- **Google Cloud Agentic Toolkit** — Expanded API coverage, Workspace integrations, and pricing details for enterprise agent deployments are expected. Google has reportedly shut down its internal "Mariner" agent project ahead of I/O, consolidating around Gemini-native agentic capabilities.

### What's Buzzing

US Treasury Secretary Bessent said last week he expects a "big step-function jump" from upcoming Gemini releases — an unusual government signal of confidence in a specific company's upcoming product.

## 💰 Anthropic's $900B Valuation Round — Progressing Toward May Close

Bloomberg reports that Anthropic's fundraising round — **at least $30 billion at a $900B+ valuation** — is expected to close as soon as the end of May. No term sheet has been signed as of May 16, but the round is actively progressing.

If it closes at $900B+, it would:
- Nearly triple Anthropic's $380B February valuation
- Place Anthropic **above OpenAI's $852B March valuation** for the first time

Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei has said the funding will go toward compute infrastructure — primarily the Amazon and Google Cloud commitments coming online through 2027.

### The 3.5GW TPU Deal

Anthropic also announced an expanded agreement to tap **3.5GW of Google's TPU capacity via Broadcom**. This builds on the October deal for 1 million of Google's TPUs. The new compute is expected to bring over 1GW of AI compute capacity online for Anthropic this year alone. A vast majority will be situated in the US, expanding on Anthropic's $50B commitment to domestic computing infrastructure.

Anthropic's revenue run rate has already surpassed **$30 billion** — up from roughly $9 billion at the end of 2025.

## 🥑 Meta Avocado — May Window Closing With No Announcement

Reuters sources said in April that Meta Avocado would land "May or June." With May now two-thirds over and no announcement, the window is closing.

Internal tests reportedly show Avocado performing **between Gemini 2.5 and Gemini 3.0** — below the threshold needed to credibly compete with GPT-5.5 or Claude Opus 4.7 on developer benchmarks.

Meta faces an awkward choice:
- **Announce before I/O** → get buried by Google's news cycle
- **Announce the same week** → invite direct Gemini 4.0 comparison
- **Slip to June** → the most likely outcome, but still awkward

With Google I/O on May 19 set to dominate tech headlines, a June launch for Avocado remains the most probable scenario.

## 📱 OpenAI AI-First Device Rumors Resurface

Reports this week cited growing signals that OpenAI is exploring an **"AI-first device"** — potentially eliminating traditional app interfaces entirely in favor of an always-on AI layer. The project, if real, would put OpenAI in direct competition with Apple's hardware ecosystem.

OpenAI has partnerships with MediaTek and Qualcomm for chip supply — the same partners named in earlier smartphone rumors from May 1. No announcement has been made, and OpenAI has not confirmed any device project publicly.

## 🛡️ AI Regulation: All Five Frontier Labs Sign CAISI Agreements

The US Commerce Department's CAISI (Center for AI Safety and Innovation) now has **pre-deployment AI evaluation agreements** with all five frontier labs:

1. **OpenAI**
2. **Anthropic**
3. **Google DeepMind**
4. **Microsoft**
5. **xAI**

These agreements give CAISI advance access to evaluate frontier models before public deployment — a significant step toward government oversight of the most powerful AI systems.

Meanwhile, the EU is in discussions with Anthropic about Mythos access but has not reached the same stage as its OpenAI agreement. The UK's AI Safety Institute published updated guidance on red-teaming frontier models ahead of I/O, signaling coordinated international attention on model capabilities.
