## US Government Forces Anthropic to Shut Down Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5

This is the biggest story of the week — maybe the year. On June 12, the US government issued an **export control directive** that forced Anthropic to **shut down both Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for all customers worldwide**.

### What happened

- The directive, citing national security authorities, **prohibits all foreign nationals from accessing Fable 5 and Mythos 5** — including foreign Anthropic employees working inside the United States
- Because the models cannot practically be restricted to US-only users, Anthropic said the "net effect" is that they must **abruptly disable both models for all customers** to ensure compliance
- Anthropic reviewed a demonstration of the alleged jailbreak technique the government cited and found only **"a small number of previously known, minor vulnerabilities"** — noting that other publicly available models, including OpenAI's GPT-5.5, can discover the same issues without any bypass
- The government **offered no explanation** of the underlying national security rationale, according to Quartz reporting

### Why this matters

This isn't happening in a vacuum. It's the latest escalation in an ongoing conflict between Anthropic and parts of the US security bureaucracy:

- The US Defense Department previously designated Anthropic a **"supply chain risk"** — the first time a US company has received such a designation
- Anthropic is currently **suing the Pentagon** over that designation
- Just days before this directive, there were signs that tensions between Anthropic and the Trump administration were easing ahead of Anthropic's IPO
- The shutdown effectively removes Anthropic's most capable models from the market at a critical moment — right as the company prepares to go public

### The broader implications

This is uncharted territory. For the first time, the US government has used export control authority to **force the shutdown of a frontier AI model** — not just restrict its sale abroad, but take it offline entirely. If the government can do this to Anthropic today, it can do it to any AI company tomorrow. The precedent is seismic.

> Wired's headline captured it plainly: **"Anthropic Says It's Taking Claude Fable 5 Offline to Comply With US Government Order"**

## OpenAI Confidentially Files for IPO — Valued at Up to $852 Billion

OpenAI has officially joined the IPO race. On **June 8**, the company confirmed it had **confidentially submitted a draft S-1 registration statement** to the SEC, just one week after Anthropic did the same.

### The numbers

- OpenAI was last valued at **$852 billion** in private markets, with some projections reaching toward **$1 trillion**
- The filing doesn't mean an immediate listing — it allows regulators to review documents privately while OpenAI prepares
- CNBC reports that OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google are all named as **SpaceX's "key competitors" in AI** in SpaceX's own S-1 filing
- The timing puts OpenAI slightly behind Anthropic in the IPO pipeline, but both are racing toward public markets as compute costs and investor demand continue climbing

### Why it matters

Two of the three biggest AI companies going public within weeks of each other? That's a defining moment for the industry. The IPO filings will finally reveal the financial realities behind the hype — revenue, losses, compute spend, and whether these companies can sustain their growth trajectories on public market scrutiny.

## Google Signs $920M/Month Compute Deal with SpaceX

The AI compute wars just got a new front. **Google will pay SpaceX $920 million per month** to lease approximately **110,000 GPUs** from xAI's data centers, according to a regulatory filing made by SpaceX on June 5.

### Deal details

- The agreement runs from **October 2026 through June 2029**
- If SpaceX fails to deliver the GPUs by end of September, Google can terminate or accept a prorated reduction
- Google characterized this as **"a short-term, timely agreement"** to provide "bridge capacity" for surging Gemini Enterprise demand
- This comes on top of Anthropic's **$1.25 billion/month** deal for the entire Colossus 1 data center (300+ megawatts, 220,000+ GPUs)

### What's really happening

SpaceX/xAI clearly overbuilt compute capacity and is now monetizing it aggressively ahead of its own IPO. The combined revenue from these two deals alone — **over $2 billion per month** — turns xAI's data center business into one of the largest cloud computing operations in the world. And Google, which operates its own massive data center fleet, is admitting it doesn't have enough capacity to meet demand for Gemini. That tells you everything about where AI infrastructure is headed.

## Moonshot AI's Kimi K2.7-Code Tops Coding Benchmarks

China's Moonshot AI released **Kimi K2.7-Code**, a specialized coding model that reports **21.8% on Kimi-Code Bench V2**, surpassing the previous K2.6 model. The release comes as Moonshot AI's valuation reportedly approaches **$30 billion**, making it one of China's most valuable AI companies.

### What makes K2.7-Code notable

- Built specifically for **code generation and programming tasks**, not as a general-purpose model
- Joins an increasingly competitive coding AI space where Microsoft's Copilot, OpenAI's Codex, and Anthropic's Claude are all vying for developer mindshare
- Represents China's continued push into frontier AI despite US export controls on chips and models
