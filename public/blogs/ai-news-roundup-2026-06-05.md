## ⚖️ D.C. Circuit Calls Pentagon's Anthropic Blacklisting "Spectacular Overreach"

The legal battle between Anthropic and the Trump administration reached its most dramatic moment yet on May 19, when a three-judge panel of the **D.C. Circuit Court of Appeals** heard oral arguments over the Pentagon's designation of Anthropic as a "supply chain risk" — and one judge didn't mince words.

### What Happened in Court

During the two-hour hearing, the panel pressed both sides hard, but the Pentagon faced the sharpest questioning. Judge **Karen LeCraft Henderson** described the government's designation as a **"spectacular overreach,"** questioning whether the Defense Department had any evidence that Anthropic actually posed a supply chain security risk.

The core issue: The Pentagon labeled Anthropic a supply chain risk — a designation typically reserved for **foreign adversaries** — after the company refused to allow its Claude models to be used for **autonomous lethal weapons and mass surveillance**. Anthropic argues this was retaliation for exercising its First Amendment rights, not a genuine national security determination.

### The Two-Track Legal War

This case is playing out across two courts simultaneously:

- **N.D. California (Judge Rita Lin):** Issued a preliminary injunction on March 26 blocking the supply chain risk designation. Judge Lin called the government's theory "Orwellian." The government appealed to the Ninth Circuit, where it's pending.
- **D.C. Circuit (FASCSA designation):** On April 8, the same three-judge panel denied Anthropic's emergency motion for a stay, allowing the FASCSA designation to remain in effect. But the court granted expedited review and scheduled the May 19 oral arguments.

The irony? Even as the government fights to maintain the blacklisting, it's reportedly exploring how to adopt Anthropic's most powerful model — **Mythos** — for cybersecurity threat detection. You can't make this up.

### Where Things Stand

The D.C. Circuit's ruling on the merits is expected in the coming weeks. Meanwhile, Anthropic has also filed to go public with an S-1 at a valuation reportedly exceeding $65 billion, making this legal showdown a defining test of whether AI companies can set ethical boundaries without government retaliation.

### Why It Matters

This is the most consequential legal case in AI policy history. If the courts ultimately side with the Pentagon, it sends a clear signal: the government can weaponize procurement law to punish AI companies that refuse military work. If Anthropic prevails, it establishes that AI safety commitments carry legal protection. The entire industry is watching.

## 🍁 Canada Launches "AI for All" — A $200B Sovereign AI Strategy

Prime Minister **Mark Carney** unveiled **"AI for All,"** Canada's new national artificial intelligence strategy on June 4, positioning the country as a leader among middle powers building sovereign AI capability.

### The Numbers

- **$200 billion** in targeted economic growth
- **250,000 new AI-related jobs** created
- **90,000 AI jobs and work placements** for young Canadians
- **Up to $1 billion** to build a **public AI supercomputer** through the AI Sovereign Compute Infrastructure (SCIP) Program
- Free **AI literacy training** for all Canadians

### The Strategy Pillars

The plan is built around several key themes:

- **Sovereign AI infrastructure:** A public AI supercomputer to ensure Canadian researchers, government, and industry aren't dependent on U.S. compute providers. The Canadian Sovereign AI Compute Strategy commits up to $1 billion for this purpose.
- **Talent retention:** The strategy explicitly acknowledges the "uncomfortable reality" that the U.S. has been a more attractive place for Canadian AI pioneers, and aims to reverse the brain drain.
- **Privacy and consumer protection:** New legislation to protect Canadians against deepfakes, strengthen privacy, and govern AI responsibly.
- **Enterprise adoption:** Support for businesses adopting AI, with Cognizant's AI Forum (see below) as one example of the ecosystem building around this.

### The Context

Canada is home to three world-class National AI Institutes: the **Vector Institute** (Toronto), **Mila** (Montréal), and the **Alberta Machine Intelligence Institute** (Edmonton). But the country has historically ridden the tailwinds of the United States on tech policy. Carney made the sovereignty pitch explicit: *"Prosperity and sovereignty in the age of AI belong to nations that can build, adopt and govern AI on their own terms."*

The strategy also arrives against the backdrop of President Trump's continued references to Canada as the "51st state," adding a geopolitical urgency to the sovereign compute push.

### Why It Matters

Canada is the first major middle-power country to articulate a comprehensive sovereign AI strategy with concrete infrastructure commitments. If it delivers on the public supercomputer and talent retention goals, it could become the template for other nations — from Europe to Southeast Asia — looking to build AI capability without becoming entirely dependent on U.S. or Chinese infrastructure. But as the EU's own struggling €20 billion AI gigafactory plan shows, announcements are easier than execution.

## 💊 Chai Discovery: The $1.3B AI Drug Discovery Startup in Talks to Raise $400M More

AI drug discovery startup **Chai Discovery** is in talks to raise an additional **$400 million at a $3.4 billion valuation**, according to a Forbes report published June 4. That would nearly triple the company's $1.3 billion valuation from its Series B just months ago.

### What Chai Discovery Does

Chai Discovery builds **generative AI models for antibody and protein design**. Its flagship model, **Chai-2**, is a zero-shot, de novo antibody design system that can generate new molecular binders without relying on known antibodies or target-specific tuning. When it launched last summer, the company's cofounder Jack Dent told Forbes: *"It was like we dropped a bomb on the field. People were messaging on LinkedIn at 2 a.m., saying, 'I am so excited I can't sleep.'"*

### The Big Pharma Bets

Chai Discovery is already working with **Pfizer** and **Eli Lilly**, two of the world's largest pharmaceutical companies. Monoclonal antibodies account for roughly 25% of new drug approvals, and traditional antibody discovery is slow and expensive. Chai's AI approach promises to compress timelines from years to months.

### The Funding Trajectory

- **August 2025:** $70 million Series A + Chai-2 launch
- **Late 2025:** $130 million Series B at $1.3 billion valuation (co-led by Oak HC/FT and General Catalyst)
- **June 2026:** In talks for $400 million at $3.4 billion valuation (lead investor not yet chosen)

### Why It Matters

The AI drug discovery space is heating up rapidly. Google DeepMind's **Isomorphic Labs** is reportedly raising over $2 billion. Chai Discovery's "portable AI platform" approach — avoiding tight lab integration in favor of licensing models to pharma partners — is a different bet than competitors like Insilico Medicine (which just signed a $2.8B deal with Eli Lilly). If the $3.4B round closes, it would validate the thesis that AI-first drug discovery companies can command pharma-scale valuations before a single drug reaches market.

## 🤖 OpenAI Workspace Agents: Custom GPTs Are Being Phased Out

OpenAI's **Workspace Agents**, launched on April 22, are signaling the end of the **Custom GPT** era for business users — and the transition is happening faster than most people realize.

### What Are Workspace Agents?

Workspace Agents are **Codex-powered AI assistants** that run persistently in the cloud, retain context across projects, and can take actions in third-party enterprise tools like **Slack, Google Drive, Microsoft apps, Salesforce, Notion, and Atlassian Rovo**. Unlike Custom GPTs, which primarily responded to individual conversational requests, Workspace Agents can autonomously execute multi-step workflows.

### The Phase-Out

OpenAI explicitly describes Workspace Agents as an **"evolution of GPTs"** and has announced plans to actively facilitate the transition:

- Existing Custom GPTs will be **convertible directly into Workspace Agents**
- Custom GPTs for business accounts are being **deprecated** — the Custom GPT era lasted just 18 months
- The shutdown date for Custom GPTs on business accounts is **August 26, 2026**
- Workspace Agents are currently in **Research Preview**, available free to Business, Enterprise, Edu, and Teachers plans

### What's Different

| Feature | Custom GPTs | Workspace Agents |
|---------|------------|-----------------|
| Runtime | Per-conversation | Persistent cloud |
| Integrations | Limited (Actions) | Slack, Salesforce, Notion, Google Drive, etc. |
| Autonomy | Responds to prompts | Executes multi-step workflows |
| Team sharing | Manual | Built-in org-wide sharing |
| Underlying engine | GPT models | Codex |

### Why It Matters

This is a quiet but significant pivot. Custom GPTs were OpenAI's first attempt at "AI for everyone" — roughly 2 million people built them. But they were fundamentally limited: a saved prompt with a personality and maybe a custom Action. Workspace Agents represent OpenAI's real enterprise play: AI that doesn't just chat but **does work** across your entire tool stack. The 18-month lifespan of Custom GPTs also serves as a cautionary tale for anyone building on OpenAI's platform — today's flagship feature can become tomorrow's deprecated API.
