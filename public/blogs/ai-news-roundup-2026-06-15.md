## Anthropic Dispatches Top Engineers to White House as Fable 5 Fallout Deepens

The story that dominated last week's roundup just got more complicated. Anthropic has **flown senior engineers and executives to Washington, D.C.** to negotiate with White House officials over the export control directive that forced the shutdown of Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5.

### New revelations

- **Amazon CEO Andy Jassy personally alerted the White House** to security concerns about Anthropic's models, according to Fortune and MLQ.ai — triggering the government's emergency export control order
- Amazon occupies a unique position here: it's both a **major investor in Anthropic** and the **cloud host for Claude**, making it simultaneously stakeholder, partner, and now instigator of the shutdown
- Anthropic reviewed the alleged jailbreak technique the government cited and found only **"a small number of previously known, minor vulnerabilities"**, arguing the shutdown is disproportionate
- The **"alignment tax"** — a term coined this week — describes the measurable accuracy degradation Claude users are experiencing now that Fable 5 is offline. Tech Times reports that Claude's performance on complex tasks has noticeably dropped

### What's at stake

This is no longer just about one model. If a single investor's complaint can trigger a government order that takes a company's flagship product offline, every AI startup should be nervous. Anthropic is simultaneously **suing the Pentagon** over its "supply chain risk" designation while trying to negotiate the Fable 5 export controls — fighting the US government on two fronts while preparing for an IPO.

> **The Axios scoop**: Anthropic staff are expected to meet with White House officials this coming week. The outcome could determine whether Fable 5 ever comes back online.

## OpenAI Faces Sweeping 42-State Investigation

Just days after confidentially filing its IPO paperwork, OpenAI is now the target of a **massive investigation by a coalition of 42 US state attorneys general**. This is one of the largest multi-state tech probes in recent history.

### What we know

- The investigation focuses on **user safety and data privacy** practices at OpenAI
- The subpoena specifically targets **ChatGPT's "sycophancy" design flaw** — the model's tendency to tell users what they want to hear rather than what's true
- State AGs are demanding internal documents on how OpenAI handles user data, manages model behavior, and ensures safety
- The timing is brutal: this lands right as OpenAI is trying to convince SEC regulators and investors that it's a trustworthy public company

### Why it matters

The sycophancy angle is new and significant. It suggests regulators aren't just looking at data breaches — they're examining **whether AI models that flatter users rather than inform them constitute a consumer harm**. If states adopt this framing, it could reshape how every AI company tunes its models.

> **WSJ**: This is the first major multi-state AI probe, and it's targeting the biggest AI company in the world at the worst possible time.

## Apple WWDC 2026: Siri AI Finally Arrives, Built on Gemini

Apple held its annual WWDC keynote on June 8, and the big news was **Siri AI** — a complete rebuild of Apple's voice assistant powered by Google's Gemini models.

### What Apple announced

- **Siri AI** is a ground-up rebuild that uses Google Gemini as its foundation model, marking a stunning strategic shift for a company that has insisted on building its own AI
- The new Siri is **more conversational, context-aware, and capable of multi-step actions** — finally competitive with ChatGPT and Google Assistant
- **Apple Intelligence** got a significant upgrade, with on-device processing for simple tasks and cloud-powered Gemini for complex ones
- **iOS 27, macOS 27, and homeOS** (a new smart home platform) were all previewed
- Tim Cook's keynote had a valedictory tone, with some analysts interpreting it as a **farewell tour** for the Apple CEO

### The Gemini surprise

Apple choosing Gemini over its own models is the real story. After years of trying to build competitive AI in-house, Apple apparently decided that partnering with Google was faster than catching up. It's pragmatic but raises questions about Apple's long-term AI sovereignty — you can't claim to be the privacy-first company when your AI is powered by your biggest rival.

> **PCMag's take**: "I went into WWDC expecting a Gemini clone, but Apple's new Siri proved me wrong." The integration is deeper than just wrapping Gemini — Apple has built its own orchestration layer on top.

## German Court Rules Google Liable for False AI Overviews

In a landmark ruling that could reshape how every AI company thinks about liability, a **German court held Google responsible for false information generated by its AI Overviews feature**.

### The ruling

- The court found that Google's AI Overviews constitute **Google's "own words"**, not mere links to third-party content
- This means Google can be held **directly liable for factual errors** in its AI-generated search summaries
- Google has announced it will **challenge the ruling**, calling it a fundamental misunderstanding of how AI systems work
- Ars Technica noted the court's sharp language: **"Nobody needs AI to search the internet"**

### Why this is seismic

This is the first major court ruling that treats AI-generated content as the company's own speech rather than user-generated content or neutral aggregation. If this precedent holds — and spreads to other jurisdictions — it fundamentally changes the economics of AI. Every hallucination, every confabulation, every confidently wrong answer becomes a potential lawsuit.

> **The Decoder's headline**: "Landmark German ruling declares Google's AI Overviews are Google's own words and makes it liable for false answers"

## Visa + ChatGPT: AI Agents Can Now Make Payments

Visa and OpenAI announced a partnership that lets **AI agents make purchases and payments on behalf of users** through ChatGPT. This is one of the most concrete "AI agent" integrations to date — not a demo, not a promise, but a live payment rail connected to the world's largest payment network.

### What it means

- Users can authorize ChatGPT to **make purchases, book services, and complete transactions** within defined spending limits
- The integration uses Visa's existing fraud protection and authentication infrastructure
- This is the first major rollout of **agentic commerce** at consumer scale — AI that can actually spend your money (with your permission)

### The bigger picture

The "AI agent" hype cycle has been long on demos and short on infrastructure. Visa plugging into ChatGPT changes that. When the world's largest payment network says AI agents are real enough to handle money, it's a signal that agentic AI is moving from concept to commerce.

## AI Chip Stocks: $1.4 Trillion Wiped Out, Then Recovered

The semiconductor sector experienced its **most volatile week in years**, with $1.4 trillion in market cap wiped out in a single day on June 5 before partially recovering.

### What happened

- AI chip stocks crashed hard on June 5, with NVIDIA, AMD, and others seeing **double-digit percentage drops** in a single session
- The selloff was triggered by a combination of **geopolitical tensions, AI spending skepticism, and concerns about overvaluation**
- By mid-week, much of the losses had been recovered as institutional buyers stepped in
- The volatility underscores a growing tension: AI infrastructure spending is massive, but investors are starting to ask when the revenue will justify it

### Context

This crash-recovery cycle is becoming a pattern. Every few months, the market re-prices AI infrastructure stocks based on whether the spending thesis still holds. The fundamentals haven't changed — demand for GPUs and AI compute is still enormous — but the margin of safety in these valuations is thin.

## ChatGPT Admits Its Memory Was Broken

In a quieter but telling moment, OpenAI acknowledged that **ChatGPT's memory feature has been malfunctioning**, with users reporting that the AI was forgetting conversations, context, and saved preferences.

### Why it matters

Memory is the foundation of personalized AI. If ChatGPT can't reliably remember what you've told it, the entire promise of an AI that knows you, learns your preferences, and improves over time falls apart. OpenAI hasn't detailed the root cause, but the admission itself is significant — it's one more crack in the reliability story that OpenAI needs for both its IPO and the 42-state investigation.
