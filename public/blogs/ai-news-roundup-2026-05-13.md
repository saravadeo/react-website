## 🔥 Sam Altman Takes the Stand in Musk v. OpenAI Trial

The most consequential legal battle in AI history entered its third week on Tuesday, with OpenAI CEO **Sam Altman** taking the witness stand in Oakland, California. The trial pits co-founder Elon Musk against the company he helped create, and Altman's testimony was the most anticipated moment yet.

### What Altman Said

Altman testified that **Musk wanted "total control" of OpenAI** — demanding a majority stake and pushing to fold the company into Tesla before departing in 2018. According to Altman, Musk sought 90% equity in any for-profit OpenAI entity, promising to reduce his control over time. When co-founders asked Musk what would happen to OpenAI upon his death, Altman described his answer — that he might pass it to his children — as a "hair-raising moment."

### The Core Legal Questions

The nine-person jury is weighing three claims:

1. **Breach of charitable trust** — Did Musk's ~$38M in early donations create a legal trust requiring OpenAI to remain nonprofit forever?
2. **Unjust enrichment** — Did Altman and president Greg Brockman improperly enrich themselves by converting OpenAI to for-profit?
3. **Unfair business practices** — Was the for-profit conversion a breach of duty to the original nonprofit mission?

### What's at Stake

Musk is seeking **$134 billion** — the redistribution of OpenAI's for-profit value to the nonprofit — plus Altman's removal as CEO. The trial's outcome could determine whether any mission-driven AI lab can legally convert to for-profit status, a question that reverberates across the entire industry. Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella and OpenAI board chair Bret Taylor also testified this week.

## 🛡️ Google Stops First Known AI-Developed Zero-Day Exploit

Google's Threat Intelligence Group (GTIG) disclosed on Monday that it **identified and disrupted the first known zero-day exploit developed with AI** — a watershed moment in cybersecurity.

### What Happened

A "prominent cybercrime threat actor" used an AI model to develop a zero-day vulnerability that could **bypass two-factor authentication (2FA)** on a widely-used open-source web-based system administration tool. The Python exploit script bore hallmarks consistent with LLM-generated code, including a "hallucinated CVSS score" and "structured, textbook formatting."

### How Google Stopped It

GTIG detected the exploit before it could be deployed at scale and **alerted the vendor**, who patched the vulnerability before mass exploitation could begin. Google emphasized this is likely "the tip of the iceberg" — but also demonstrated that proactive AI-powered threat intelligence can catch what traditional methods might miss.

### The Bigger Picture

The report also documented how threat actors are using **"persona-driven jailbreaking"** to coax AI models into finding security vulnerabilities. While Google's Gemini was not implicated in the attack, the milestone confirms a long-feared escalation: AI is now being weaponized to discover and exploit previously unknown vulnerabilities.

## 🧠 Anthropic's Claude Agents Learn to "Dream"

At its **Code with Claude 2026** developer conference in San Francisco on May 6–7, Anthropic unveiled **"Dreaming"** — a new capability for Claude Managed Agents that lets AI agents review their past sessions while idle and extract lessons to improve future performance.

### How Dreaming Works

When a Claude Managed Agent finishes a task, it enters a "dreaming" phase where it:

- Reviews completed sessions to identify patterns and mistakes
- Updates its working memory with distilled lessons
- Builds a continuous improvement loop that requires **no human intervention**

The result: agents that get better at their jobs over time, autonomously — an idea Anthropic co-founder Jack Clark connected to the broader trend of AI self-improvement.

### Three Features Moved to Public Beta

Alongside Dreaming, Anthropic moved two other features to public beta:

1. **Outcomes** — A rubric-based grading system where a separate AI agent scores output quality. Anthropic reported a **10.1% improvement** on hard PowerPoint generation tasks using Outcomes.
2. **Multi-Agent Orchestration** — A lead agent can now decompose tasks and delegate subtasks to specialist agents, each with its own model, system prompt, and context. Harvey reported **6× productivity gains**, and Netflix ran **hundreds of builds in parallel**.

## 🚀 Anthropic–SpaceX Colossus Deal: The Irony Writes Itself

In the "you can't make this up" department: **Elon Musk's SpaceX is now providing compute infrastructure to Anthropic** — the same week Musk is suing OpenAI for betraying its nonprofit mission.

### The Deal

Announced May 6, Anthropic signed an agreement to use **all compute capacity at SpaceX's Colossus 1 data center** in Memphis, Tennessee — over **220,000 Nvidia GPUs** and **300 megawatts** of power. The deal was struck through SpaceXAI (xAI's infrastructure arm), and gives Anthropic immediate access to one of the world's largest AI compute clusters.

### Why It Matters

- Anthropic's Claude Code usage grew **80× in Q1 2026**, straining capacity. The Colossus deal doubles rate limits for all paid Claude tiers.
- Musk called Anthropic "evil" three months ago. Now he's renting them his supercomputer.
- The two companies also expressed interest in developing **orbital AI compute capacity** — data centers in space powered by near-constant solar energy, delivered via SpaceX's Starship rockets.

Beyond SpaceX, Anthropic has secured 5GW from Amazon, another 5GW from Google/Broadcom (arriving 2027), $30B in Azure capacity through Microsoft and Nvidia, and a $50B U.S. infrastructure investment with Fluidstack.
