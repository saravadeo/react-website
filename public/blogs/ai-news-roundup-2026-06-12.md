SpaceX begins trading on the Nasdaq today in what could be the largest IPO in history. OpenAI just filed its own confidential S-1. The EU AI Act's Code of Practice is now in effect. And OpenAI quietly embedded Codex into ChatGPT with business-grade plugins. Here's what matters today.

## SpaceX IPO Day: Largest Public Offering in History Begins Trading

SpaceX begins trading on the Nasdaq today under ticker **SPCX** at a fixed price of **$135 per share**, targeting a **$1.75 trillion valuation** — making it the largest IPO in history by a wide margin, surpassing Saudi Aramco's $29.4 billion debut by more than 2.5 times.

### The numbers

- **$75 billion raised** at the base offering size, with a greenshoe option that could push it to $1.9T
- **$18.67 billion in 2025 revenue**, up 33% from $14.1B in 2024
- **30% allocated to retail investors** — an unusually large tranche aimed at tapping Elon Musk's following
- **Early Nasdaq-100 inclusion** reportedly negotiated as a listing condition, which could trigger forced passive buying within days
- **Fixed price of $135** rather than a traditional range — an unusual take-it-or-leave-it approach

### The xAI problem buried in the S-1

The bull case for SpaceX rests on Starlink and launch dominance. The bear case is all xAI:

- **xAI lost $2.47 billion** on $818 million in revenue in Q1 2026 — a staggering burn rate
- **Grok adoption is weak**: just 3 federal use cases out of 400+ disclosed AI deployments, despite a GSA contract making Grok available at $0.42 per agency
- **SpaceX spent $2.8 billion on gas turbines** for xAI's Memphis data centers, and the S-1 explicitly warns about "nonconsensual or exploitative imagery" and misinformation from Grok's less-restricted output modes
- **Anthropic will pay SpaceX $1.25 billion/month** through May 2029 for compute at the Colossus 1 data center — a curious arrangement where one AI company funds another's infrastructure
- **xAI also secured a $200 million Pentagon contract** for "Grok for Government," despite minimal actual federal adoption

Morningstar placed a **$780 billion fair value estimate** on SpaceX — roughly 56% below the IPO price — arguing that the AI segment's economics don't justify the premium.

### What to watch today

- **Opening trade price**: The real price discovery begins when SPCX starts trading. A pop validates the AI-infrastructure thesis and could boost OpenAI and Anthropic's IPO prospects. A struggle could cool the entire AI IPO pipeline
- **Retail investor behavior**: With 30% allocated to retail — far more than typical IPOs — the first-day performance will reflect Main Street sentiment as much as Wall Street's
- **xAI restructuring signals**: Musk is splitting xAI into four units (Grok, Coding, Imagine, "Macro Hard") — an attempt to clarify a division that has lacked focus

**Why it matters:** The SpaceX IPO isn't just about rockets. It's a referendum on whether AI infrastructure companies can justify multi-trillion-dollar valuations before proving sustainable revenue — and it sets the tone for OpenAI and Anthropic's own public market debuts.

## OpenAI Files Confidential S-1 for IPO

On June 8, OpenAI **confidentially filed its S-1 with the SEC**, joining Anthropic (which filed June 1) and setting up a potential three-way AI IPO collision in the second half of 2026.

### Key details

- **Targeting a $730–850 billion valuation**, with Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley as lead underwriters
- **IPO could come as soon as September 2026**, though timing remains fluid
- **The filing comes days after OpenAI won its lawsuit against Elon Musk**, who had claimed the company illegally converted from nonprofit to for-profit. An advisory jury found Musk waited too long to bring his claims
- **CFO Sarah Friar** described the IPO as "good hygiene" for a company of OpenAI's size in an April interview

### What makes this different

OpenAI's IPO registration will mark the **first time its actual revenue and cost structure are disclosed publicly**. Given that the company reportedly operates at significant losses while investing billions in compute, this will give investors and the industry the first hard data point against which AI private-market valuations can be measured.

The competitive dynamic is intense: three companies worth a combined $3.5+ trillion going public in one year is unprecedented. Institutional investor appetite will be tested, and simultaneous filings from OpenAI and Anthropic could divide demand, leaving one or both listings undersubscribed.

**Why it matters:** OpenAI's IPO filing is the second act in the AI public-markets story that SpaceX opens today. The question isn't whether these companies can go public — it's whether public markets can absorb all three without repricing the entire sector.

## EU AI Act: Code of Practice Takes Effect

The **EU AI Act's Code of Practice** is now in effect as of June 2026, establishing binding requirements for general-purpose AI (GPAI) models and the companies that deploy them in Europe.

### What the Code covers

- **AI-generated content labeling**: All AI-generated text, images, audio, and video must be clearly disclosed to European users
- **GPAI model transparency**: Providers of general-purpose AI models must publish detailed documentation about training data, compute, and capabilities
- **Risk assessment requirements**: Systematic risk assessments for frontier models, including evaluation of potential misuse
- **Copyright compliance**: GPAI providers must implement copyright retention signals and provide rights holders with tools to opt out of training data usage

### Enforcement reality

While the Code is technically in effect, full enforcement with penalties doesn't kick in until August 2026. That gives companies a grace period — but the rules are clear about what's coming. OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and Meta all operate in the EU and will need to comply.

The labeling requirement is particularly significant: it means that **every piece of AI-generated content served to European users must be identifiable as such**. This has massive implications for how companies like OpenAI (ChatGPT), Anthropic (Claude), and Google (Gemini) deploy their products in the EU.

**Why it matters:** The EU AI Act is the world's first comprehensive AI regulation with enforcement teeth. The Code of Practice establishes the practical playbook for how these rules work in practice — and every AI company operating in Europe has to comply or face fines up to 7% of global revenue.

## OpenAI Puts Codex in ChatGPT with 6 Business Plugins

OpenAI has rolled out **Codex inside ChatGPT** alongside **six business-specific plugins**, extending its agentic coding platform from a standalone tool to an integrated part of the ChatGPT ecosystem.

### The six plugins

The business plugins cover 62 enterprise applications across six domains:

- **Sales & CRM**: Pipeline management, lead scoring, and automated outreach
- **Finance**: Expense categorization, budget tracking, and financial reporting
- **HR & Operations**: Onboarding workflows, policy compliance, and document processing
- **Engineering**: Code review automation, test generation, and deployment assistance
- **Marketing**: Content generation, campaign analytics, and A/B testing
- **Legal**: Contract analysis, compliance checking, and clause extraction

### Codex Sites: Prompt-to-Deployed App

OpenAI also launched **Codex Sites** on June 2 — a prompt-to-deployed-app builder available to Business and Enterprise customers. The tool lets users describe an application in natural language and have Codex build, deploy, and host it. OpenAI is offering **2 months free Codex Enterprise** to companies switching from competitors.

### The agentic shift

This is the clearest signal yet that OpenAI sees its future not in chatbots but in **agents that execute work**. Codex isn't just code generation — it's a programmable agent platform with access to business systems. The six plugins turn ChatGPT from a conversation tool into an operations layer.

The competitive landscape is now a three-way race for the terminal/agent developer tool:
- **Claude Code** (Anthropic) — first-mover in agentic coding
- **Codex** (OpenAI) — now embedded inside ChatGPT with business context
- **Antigravity** (Google) — replacing Gemini CLI with a full agent platform

**Why it matters:** OpenAI isn't just building a better chatbot. Codex + business plugins + Codex Sites is a full-stack agent platform. The question isn't whether AI can write code — it's whether AI can run your business processes. OpenAI is betting yes.
