# AI News Roundup — May 20, 2026

Google I/O 2026 wrapped up today with a second day of announcements that shifted the focus from headline models to the agentic layer — and the stakes got real. Gemini 3.5 Flash went live, Gemini Spark promised 24/7 personal AI agents, and Antigravity 2.0 turned developers' heads. Meanwhile, away from Mountain View, Anthropic quietly passed a historic milestone: more businesses now pay for Claude than for ChatGPT. Here's your May 20 briefing.

---

## 🔥 Google I/O Day 2: The Agentic Layer Arrives

If Day 1 was about Gemini 4 flexing model muscle, Day 2 was about *what those models can actually do*. Google went all-in on agents, automation, and developer tools — signaling that the AI wars are shifting from benchmark battles to workflow takeovers.

### Gemini 3.5 Flash: Fast, Agentic, and Available Now

Google launched **Gemini 3.5 Flash** at general availability today, calling it their "strongest agentic and coding model." Available immediately in the Gemini app and across Google products, 3.5 Flash reportedly outperforms the previous Gemini 3.1 Pro on most benchmarks while delivering 4x faster inference speeds. It's also now powering Google Search and the new AI Mode. A more powerful **Gemini 3.5 Pro** is coming in June.

### Gemini Omni: One Model to Rule All Media

**Gemini Omni** debuted as Google's unified multimodal model, capable of generating and editing text, images, and video in a single model. Omni Flash is available starting today, with the full model rolling out soon. The demo showed real-time video generation and editing — a direct shot at OpenAI's Sora and Adobe's Firefly.

### Gemini Spark: Your 24/7 AI Agent

Perhaps the most headline-grabbing announcement was **Gemini Spark**, Google's "always-on" personal AI agent. Spark lives on Google's cloud VMs, working in the background to handle tasks proactively — booking restaurants, managing your calendar, researching topics, and taking actions across Google services without you asking. It launches next week exclusively for **AI Ultra** subscribers.

### Antigravity 2.0: Google's Agentic Developer Platform

**Antigravity 2.0** is Google's expanded agentic coding and automation framework, now available across desktop, CLI, SDK, and API. It lets developers build, schedule, and run AI-powered workflows — from automated code reviews to multi-step research agents. Managed Agents in the Gemini API let you deploy persistent agents that operate across tools and services. This is Google's answer to Cursor, Claude Code, and GitHub Copilot Workspace — but deeply integrated with Google Cloud.

### AI Ultra Plan: Now $100/Month (Down from $250)

Google slashed the price of its **AI Ultra** subscription from $250/month to $100/month, making it far more competitive with ChatGPT Plus ($20) and Claude Pro ($20). Ultra subscribers get 20x higher usage limits than the Pro plan, access to Gemini Spark agents, YouTube Premium included, and priority access to the most capable models. It's a clear play to expand the paying user base.

### Googlebook: Laptops Built for Gemini

Google introduced **Googlebook** — a new category of laptops "built from the ground up for Gemini Intelligence." Running on the newly rebranded **Aluminum OS** (the merged Android + ChromeOS), Googlebooks feature a Magic Pointer for contextual AI suggestions, custom widgets for task organization, and seamless file syncing with Android phones. The first devices ship this fall from multiple OEM partners.

### Google + Blackstone: TPU Cloud Joint Venture

In a strategic infrastructure play, Google and private equity giant Blackstone announced a **joint venture to create a dedicated TPU cloud**. The new company will offer Google's custom TPU chips (including the new TPU 8t and TPU 8i announced at I/O) as a cloud service, giving enterprises direct access to Google's AI accelerators without going through Google Cloud's full stack. It's a hedge against NVIDIA's GPU monopoly and a signal that Google wants TPU access to be as easy as renting A100s.

## 📊 Anthropic Surpasses OpenAI in Business Adoption

For the first time, **Anthropic has more business customers than OpenAI**, according to the May 2026 Ramp AI Index. The data shows 34.4% of businesses on Ramp's platform now pay for Anthropic services, edging past OpenAI's 32.3%.

A year ago, Anthropic sat at just 9%. That's a 26-percentage-point climb in twelve months — driven largely by the explosive adoption of Claude Code and Claude's enterprise integrations. Uber's CTO recently revealed the company blew its entire 2026 AI budget in just four months, primarily on Claude Code and Cursor, with engineers spending $500–$2,000 per person per month on API costs.

The milestone is symbolic more than it is definitive — OpenAI still likely leads in total revenue — but it marks a genuine inflection point. Ramp's economist Ara Kharazian noted that choosing an AI model is becoming "more like the green bubble/blue bubble distinction in iMessage: a signal of identity as much as a choice of technology."

## 📊 Quick Hits

- **Gemma 4** — Google released the latest version of its open-source model family, with variants ranging from lightweight to full-size, built for advanced reasoning and agentic workflows. Already available on Hugging Face.
- **Gemini Neural Expressive** — Google unveiled a new design language for the Gemini app, rolling out today on desktop, iOS, and Android. It's more conversational and context-aware.
- **Ask YouTube** — A new conversational search feature for YouTube Premium users that lets you ask questions about video content and get AI-generated answers. Live today.
- **Universal Cart** — Google's AI-powered cross-merchant shopping feature that finds the best deals across multiple stores in a single checkout flow.
- **Gmail Live & Docs Live** — Real-time AI collaboration in Gmail and Google Docs, with Gemini suggesting edits, summaries, and actions inline.
- **Daily Brief** — A new Gemini feature that delivers a personalized morning briefing with calendar events, news, weather, and proactive suggestions.
