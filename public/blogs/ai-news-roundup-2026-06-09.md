# AI News Roundup: June 9, 2026

The AI world doesn't slow down. Just as the dust settled from last week's Anthropic IPO filing and SpaceX S-1, OpenAI has entered the ring. Apple finally showed its hand with a Siri rebuild powered by Google's Gemini. And a state attorney general just put AI safety on trial. Here's everything you need to know.

## OpenAI Confidentially Files for IPO

On June 8, **OpenAI confidentially filed its S-1 with the SEC**, joining a historic trio of mega-IPOs racing toward public markets in 2026. The filing comes just one week after Anthropic's own confidential S-1 submission and days before SpaceX's market debut.

Key details:
- **Valuation target:** Over $1 trillion, based on OpenAI's last private round ($852B post-money, March 2026)
- **Underwriters:** Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley are leading the offering
- **Timeline:** OpenAI is aiming for an IPO as early as **September 2026**, though timing remains fluid
- **Revenue context:** OpenAI has not yet turned an annual profit, raising questions about how public markets will value the ChatGPT maker
- **Legal backdrop:** The filing comes just weeks after OpenAI won a court case against Elon Musk, who had sued to block OpenAI's for-profit transition — Musk has vowed to appeal

The competitive dynamics are fascinating. Anthropic filed first (June 1), but OpenAI's move suggests neither wants to be second to the public markets. As Fortune noted, Anthropic's S-1 signals it "intends to beat OpenAI to the public markets," but OpenAI clearly isn't letting that happen without a fight.

**Why it matters:** Three AI-adjacent companies — Anthropic (~$965B), OpenAI (~$852B+), and SpaceX (~$1.77T) — could raise over $200 billion combined from public markets this year. That's not just a liquidity event. It's a fundamental reshaping of who owns AI's future — and who bears the risk.

## Apple WWDC 2026: Siri AI Is Finally Here — And It's Powered by Gemini

Apple's WWDC 2026 keynote on June 8 delivered what two years of promises had been building toward: **Siri AI**, a completely rebuilt assistant that finally makes Siri competitive with ChatGPT, Google Assistant, and Claude.

### What Siri AI Can Do Now

The new Siri is a fundamental architectural shift, not an incremental upgrade:

- **Conversational intelligence:** Siri can now handle multi-step, context-aware conversations — remembering what you said three messages ago and acting across multiple apps
- **On-screen awareness:** Siri can see what's on your screen and act on it. Looking at a recipe? Ask Siri to add the ingredients to your grocery list. Reading an email? Tell Siri to draft a reply
- **Standalone app + Spotlight integration:** Siri AI lives in its own app now, and is also accessible through Spotlight search and within other apps like Photos
- **App Intents replace SiriKit:** Apple is sunsetting the old SiriKit framework in favor of App Intents, giving developers a more powerful way to connect their apps to Siri
- **Cross-app context awareness:** Siri can connect information across apps — your calendar, messages, photos, and more — to actually complete tasks, not just answer trivia

### The Gemini Factor

Here's the twist: **Siri AI is powered by Google's Gemini running on Nvidia Blackwell GPUs in Apple's Private Cloud Compute infrastructure.** Apple's on-device Foundation Models handle simpler tasks locally (preserving privacy), while Gemini handles the heavy lifting in the cloud. It's a pragmatic move — Apple acknowledged that its own models weren't enough to compete, so it partnered with the best available.

Developers get access to Apple's Foundation Models framework (now supporting image input and custom skills) and a new **Core AI** framework for running their own local models on Apple Silicon.

### Everything Else at WWDC

- **iOS 27** — Liquid Glass gets a transparency slider (Ultra Clear to Tinted Glass), AI-powered Safari tab management, one-tap password updating
- **macOS 27 "Golden Gate"** — Rebuilt Spotlight search, improved Mail search, Liquid Glass design language across the system, Siri AI accessible via Spotlight
- **Photos app** — New AI editing features letting users describe what they want to change in natural language
- **Shortcuts app** — AI-powered workflow building: describe what you want to automate and Shortcuts creates it
- **Screen Time overhaul** — New parental controls powered by AI
- **Tim Cook's farewell** — This is Cook's last WWDC as CEO before John Ternus takes over in September. He ended the keynote with a personal message about Apple's journey

**Why it matters:** Apple just went from "laggard in AI" to "the company with a billion-device distribution channel for the world's best AI models." The Gemini partnership is a seismic shift — Apple is essentially admitting it can't build the best AI alone, but it can deliver the best AI *experience* through hardware integration. Whether that's enough to challenge OpenAI and Anthropic's dominance depends entirely on execution.

## SpaceX IPO: The Largest in History Debuts June 12

SpaceX is set to begin trading on the **Nasdaq under ticker SPCX on June 12, 2026**, at **$135 per share** — valuing the company at approximately **$1.77 trillion** and raising **$74.4 billion** in the offering.

This makes it the **largest IPO in history**, surpassing Saudi Aramco's 2019 listing ($1.7T valuation, $29B raised).

Key details:
- **Revenue:** $18.67B in 2025 (33% YoY growth from $14.1B in 2024)
- **The xAI factor:** SpaceX merged with Elon Musk's AI startup xAI in February 2026 (valuing SpaceX at $1T, xAI at $250B). xAI's roughly $2.5B quarterly burn is now part of SpaceX's financials
- **Nasdaq fast-track:** SpaceX reportedly made early Nasdaq-100 inclusion a condition of listing, which could trigger forced passive buying within days of debut
- **Risk factors:** SpaceX has yet to post an annual profit. Bears point out it would have a higher market cap than Meta on revenue lower than Macy's

**Why it matters:** The SpaceX IPO isn't just about rockets. With the xAI merger, it's a combined space-infrastructure-AI play. Starlink's satellite network underpins AI's bandwidth needs. And at $1.77T, SPCX will reshape every major index it enters.

## Florida Sues OpenAI Over AI Safety

On June 1, Florida became the **first U.S. state to sue OpenAI**, with Attorney General James Uthmeier filing a lawsuit against OpenAI and CEO Sam Altman alleging the company prioritized growth over safety.

The lawsuit claims:
- OpenAI lacks effective parental controls — the free version of ChatGPT has "no gatekeeping or age verification mechanism"
- ChatGPT allegedly aided a mass shooter in the 2025 Florida State University attack (Uthmeier launched a criminal investigation in April)
- The platform provides information on self-harm and has addictive properties for young users
- OpenAI engaged in negligence and violated Florida's prohibition on unfair and deceptive practices

OpenAI responded that its safety mechanisms are robust, but the lawsuit represents a significant escalation in AI regulation. It follows similar actions against Character.AI (Kentucky and Pennsylvania lawsuits) and comes as federal AI oversight remains stalled.

**Why it matters:** This is the first state-level legal action against a major AI company by a government entity (prior suits were from individuals). If Florida succeeds, it could open the floodgates for other states — and force OpenAI to disclose internal safety practices just as it prepares for its IPO.

## Tim Cook's Last WWDC

A quieter but symbolically important moment: **Tim Cook's final WWDC keynote as Apple CEO.** Cook announced in April that he will step down in September, handing the reins to hardware chief John Ternus.

Cook ended the keynote with a personal farewell message, capping 15 years as CEO. His tenure saw Apple become the world's most valuable company, but also drew criticism for Apple Intelligence being a "big black eye" — as The Futurum Group's Dan Newman put it.

The question now: Will Ternus be more aggressive on AI, or will the Gemini partnership define Apple's AI strategy for the foreseeable future?
